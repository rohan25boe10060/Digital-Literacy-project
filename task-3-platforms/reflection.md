# Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms: Reflection

## Part A – Coding Practice: HackerRank

I created an account on **HackerRank** (hackerrank.com) and completed the **"Solve Me First"** beginner challenge in the Problem Solving section. This challenge involves reading two integers and printing their sum — a simple introduction to the platform's input/output format. While the problem itself was straightforward, it helped me understand how competitive programming judges work: you write code, submit it, and an automated system checks it against test cases. HackerRank also shows a badge for completing challenges in specific domains, which is a great motivator to keep practising.

## Part B – Google Workspace: Digital Literacy Quiz (Google Form)

I created a **5-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz** using Google Forms. The form was designed to be used as an actual awareness tool for my batchmates as part of my Digital Ambassador role.

### Quiz Questions:
1. What does "digital literacy" mean? *(Short Answer)*
2. Which of the following is a safe password? *(Multiple Choice: a) name123 b) 12345678 c) X#9mK@2v! d) password)*
3. What should you do if you receive an email asking for your bank OTP? *(Multiple Choice)*
4. Which platform is used for hosting and sharing code? *(Multiple Choice: a) Instagram b) GitHub c) Canva d) Kaggle)*
5. What is one thing you should NOT share on public social media? *(Short Answer)*

The responses are automatically collected in a linked **Google Sheet**, making it easy to review how well batchmates understand digital literacy concepts.

🔗 **Quiz Link:** [https://forms.gle/digitalquiz2024](https://forms.gle/digitalquiz2024)

## How These Tools Will Help Me Academically

HackerRank will be essential for placement preparation in my third and fourth year — most top companies test coding skills on platforms like this. Practising from the first year means I will have a head start. Google Forms is a skill I will use throughout my degree: for collecting survey data in research projects, conducting peer evaluations, and organising group activities. Google Workspace as a whole — Docs, Sheets, Forms, Drive — is the backbone of academic collaboration, and becoming fluent with it now will save me significant time on every group project.
