# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used

**Canva** (canva.com) – free browser-based design tool

## What the Infographic Shows

The infographic is titled **"Digital Literacy: Your Guide to the Digital World"** and is designed as a one-page visual resource for first-year B.Tech students. It covers three core topics:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?**  
Defined as the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using digital technology responsibly and effectively.
2. **Useful Digital Tools for Students**  
Highlights platforms like Google Workspace (Docs, Forms, Drive), GitHub for code, Canva for design, and HackerRank for coding practice.
3. **Safe Internet Practices**  
Covers using strong passwords, avoiding phishing links, keeping software updated, and not sharing personal information on unknown websites.

## Reflection

Creating this infographic was an interesting challenge. I chose Canva because it has student-friendly templates and drag-and-drop functionality that requires no design experience. The hardest part was deciding how much information to include — too much text makes an infographic unreadable, but too little leaves out important content. I ended up using icons and short bullet points rather than paragraphs, which forced me to summarise complex ideas in just a few words. I found it genuinely satisfying to see information that would usually take a whole lecture condensed into a visually appealing single page. This exercise made me realise how important visual communication is in the digital age.
