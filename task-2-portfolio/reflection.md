# Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio: Reflection

## Platforms Set Up

### 1. GitHub (github.com)
GitHub is a version control and code hosting platform used by developers worldwide. It lets you store code, collaborate on projects, track changes, and showcase your work to potential employers and internship coordinators. I created a profile README (a special repository named after my username) that includes my name, branch, year, and what I hope to learn during my B.Tech.

### 2. LinkedIn (linkedin.com)
LinkedIn is a professional networking platform used for career development, job/internship hunting, and connecting with professionals in your field. It is often the first thing recruiters check before an interview. I filled in my Education section with my B.Tech degree details, institution (VIT Bhopal University), and expected graduation year (2027).

### 3. Kaggle (kaggle.com)
Kaggle is a platform for data science and machine learning. It hosts competitions, datasets, and learning notebooks. Even as a first-year student, having a Kaggle account shows initiative in the data science domain, which is highly relevant for CSE students.

## How I Plan to Use These Platforms Over the Next Four Years

Over the next four years, I plan to use GitHub to host all my academic projects and assignments — building a portfolio that will be visible to recruiters by my final year. On LinkedIn, I will gradually add internship experiences, certifications, and projects as I complete them, and use the platform to connect with seniors and industry professionals. Kaggle will be my learning ground for data science and ML once I take up those subjects in my third year. Together, these three platforms will form the foundation of my professional digital presence as an engineer.
