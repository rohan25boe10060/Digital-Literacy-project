# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
## For College Students in India

---

### 🔐 General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols (e.g., X#9mK@2v!). Never reuse passwords across platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, banking apps, and GitHub. This adds a second layer of security even if your password is stolen.

3. **Never click on links from unknown senders** — whether in SMS, WhatsApp, email, or social media. Fraudsters use fake links that mimic official bank or government websites.

4. **Verify before you trust** — If someone calls claiming to be from your bank, SBI, or a government body, hang up and call the official helpline number listed on the bank's website.

5. **Keep your devices and apps updated** — Software updates patch security vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates for your phone's operating system and installed apps.

6. **Use only official app stores** — Download apps only from Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APK files from unknown websites.

---

### 💸 UPI & Financial Safety (Critical for Students)

7. **You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to RECEIVE money.** If anyone asks you to enter your PIN to "accept" a payment, it is 100% fraud. Immediately disconnect.

8. **Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, CVV, or net banking password with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be a bank employee, telecom agent, or scholarship officer. Legitimate institutions never ask for these details.

---

### 📱 Social Media & Account Safety

9. **Do not share your Aadhaar number, PAN, college ID, or address** on any social media platform or messaging group, even if the person seems trustworthy.

10. **Log out of shared/public computers** — If you access your accounts in a college computer lab or a friend's device, always log out completely afterward.

---

### 🚨 If You Are a Victim

11. **Report immediately** — If you have been defrauded, call the **National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** (available 24×7). Fast reporting increases the chance of freezing and recovering funds.

12. **File a complaint online** at the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)**. Keep all screenshots, transaction IDs, and call records as evidence.

---

*"The best defence against cybercrime is awareness. If something feels wrong, stop — verify — then act."*
