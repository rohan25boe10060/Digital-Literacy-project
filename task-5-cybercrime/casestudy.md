# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI/Online Payment Fraud  
**Submitted by:** Aryan Sharma | Reg. No.: 23BCE10001

\---

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India, targeting millions of smartphone users — especially students and young adults who frequently use apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm.

\---

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

**Step 1 – Initial Contact:** The victim receives a call, WhatsApp message, or SMS from someone claiming to be a bank representative, a buyer on OLX/Quikr, or a scholarship officer. The message creates urgency (e.g., "Your account will be blocked," or "Claim your scholarship now").

**Step 2 – Building Trust:** The fraudster knows some basic personal details (name, bank name) and uses this to appear legitimate. They ask the victim to "verify" their UPI ID.

**Step 3 – The Trap:** The fraudster sends a UPI *collect request* (a payment request asking the victim to approve a transfer *to* the fraudster). They tell the victim: "Just enter your PIN to receive ₹5,000." The victim, confused, enters their PIN — and money is transferred OUT of their account, not in.

**Step 4 – Disappearance:** The fraudster disconnects immediately. The money is transferred through multiple accounts and is nearly impossible to recover.

\---

## Who is Targeted?

Students selling items online, first-time UPI users, elderly people, and anyone responding to fake job or scholarship advertisements.

\---

## Consequences

Victims lose money immediately and often permanently. In some cases, fraudsters drain entire account balances. The psychological impact includes anxiety, loss of trust in digital payments, and financial stress.
