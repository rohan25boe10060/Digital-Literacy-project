# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A checklist for responsible and professional social media use.

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** — Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and write a bio that reflects who you are academically and professionally.

2. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If no, don't post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** — Review and update privacy settings on all platforms regularly. Limit who can see your personal posts.

4. **Engage positively and constructively** — Comment, share, and interact in ways that are respectful, helpful, and add value to discussions.

5. **Cite and credit others** — When sharing someone else's work, idea, or content, always give proper credit or attribution.

6. **Separate personal and professional accounts** — Consider maintaining a professional LinkedIn/GitHub presence separate from personal platforms like Instagram.

7. **Report harmful content** — Use reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, hate speech, misinformation, or inappropriate content you encounter.

8. **Verify information before sharing** — Always check the source of a news article or claim before resharing it to avoid spreading misinformation.

9. **Use strong, unique passwords** — Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all social media accounts.

10. **Curate your digital footprint intentionally** — Regularly review your past posts and remove anything that no longer represents you well.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** — Avoid posting your home address, phone number, Aadhaar/PAN details, daily schedule, or location in real time.

2. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** — Do not participate in trolling, online harassment, or group targeting. Report it if you see it.

3. **Don't post in anger or frustration** — Emotional posts about professors, classmates, or institutions can damage your reputation permanently. Write in a notes app first, wait, then decide.

4. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from unknown people** — Especially on platforms where you share personal content. Verify before connecting.

5. **Don't share exam questions, answer papers, or copyrighted material** — This can lead to academic disciplinary action and potential legal consequences.

6. **Don't use social media during class** — It disrupts your own learning and is disrespectful to educators.

7. **Don't overshare your location** — Avoid tagging your exact location in real time, especially late at night or in isolated areas.

8. **Don't click on suspicious links** — Even if they appear to come from friends. Accounts can be hacked and used to spread phishing links.

---

*This checklist is intended for first-year B.Tech students at VIT Bhopal University as part of the CSE0001 Digital Literacy course.*
